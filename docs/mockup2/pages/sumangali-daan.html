Introduction

Honour Married Women, Invoke Divine Grace

In Sanatan Dharma, a Sumangali (a married woman) represents the grace of Goddess Lakshmi and the power of Shakti—embodying prosperity, harmony, and auspiciousness in the home. Offering Seva to Sumangalis is considered a sacred act of dharmic service, ensuring abundance, protection, and spiritual upliftment for generations to come.

📖 "Donating to a Sumangali, who represents Lakshmi, is an act of sattvic charity that brings prosperity and divine grace."

— Bhagavad Gita 17.20

This Maha Sumangali Daan is a sacred way to offer gratitude, clear karmic debts, and invite divine blessings into your life. Whether performed as a personal offering, family tradition, or an act of selfless service, your contribution uplifts both the giver and the receiver, bringing peace, punya (spiritual merit), and abundance.
