Introduction

Honouring Those Who Built the Path Before Us

In the twilight years of life, what seniors seek most is not wealth, but care, respect, and dignity. Their wisdom and life experiences serve as a guiding light for younger generations, yet many elderly individuals live in loneliness, neglect, or financial hardship.

This Maha Shivratri, take the divine step of offering Daan to Senior Citizens, ensuring that they receive nourishment, warmth, and medical care, bringing them the comfort and dignity they deserve.

"That which we offer to the world returns to us manifold."

— Acharya Upendra Ji
02

Why Donate for Senior Citizens?

Express Gratitude, Show Compassion, and Earn Divine Blessings

Sanatan Dharma teaches us that serving the elderly is akin to serving our own ancestors and the Divine. The Vedas and Upanishads repeatedly emphasize that taking care of one’s elders brings immense punya (spiritual merit), inner peace, and generational blessings.

Scriptural References on Caring for the Elderly

Manusmriti (2.226) – “One who respects, serves, and provides for the elderly attains prosperity, longevity, and divine grace.”
Taittiriya Upanishad (1.11.2) – “Matru Devo Bhava, Pitru Devo Bhava” – Honor and serve your parents and elders as the Divine itself.”
Bhagavad Gita (17.20) – “Giving with sincerity, at the right time and to the right people, bestows the highest rewards.”

Many senior citizens lack basic necessities like food, medical care, and emotional support. Your generous donation can fill their final years with comfort, love, and dignity.
