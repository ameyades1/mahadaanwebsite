# Google EEAT Search Quality Rater Guidelines

## Overview

EEAT stands for **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness**. These are core principles Google uses to evaluate webpage quality, particularly for websites that could impact user safety, finances, or well-being (known as "YMYL" — Your Money or Your Life content).

EEAT is not a ranking algorithm, but rather a framework that Search Quality Raters use to evaluate content quality. Google's systems learn from these human evaluations to improve search rankings.

---

## Why EEAT Matters

- **Better User Experience**: EEAT-compliant content helps users find reliable, high-quality information
- **Trust Building**: Demonstrating expertise and authority builds reader confidence
- **Search Ranking**: Pages with strong EEAT signals tend to rank higher in Google Search
- **YMYL Impact**: Content on health, finance, legal, or safety topics is evaluated more strictly for EEAT
- **Long-term Sustainability**: Trustworthy sites outrank low-quality competitors over time

---

## 1. Experience (E)

Experience refers to the **real-world, firsthand knowledge** the content creator brings to the topic.

### Key Signals:

- **Author Biography**: Clear information about the author's background, professional experience, and qualifications
- **First-Hand Knowledge**: Content based on personal experience, case studies, or direct involvement
- **Practical Insights**: Real-world examples, lessons learned, or hands-on expertise
- **Demonstrated Success**: Track record or portfolio showing practical results
- **Professional History**: Years in the field, certifications, or proven credentials

### For Mahadaan Website:

- Feature founder/teacher bios with years of practice in yoga and spiritual traditions
- Share stories of personal transformation through the programs
- Showcase student success rates and community impact
- Display credentials and certifications of instructors

---

## 2. Expertise (X)

Expertise is the **demonstrated knowledge and skill** the creator possesses in their field.

### Key Signals:

- **Deep Knowledge**: Content shows thorough understanding of the topic
- **Detailed Information**: Goes beyond surface-level; provides comprehensive, nuanced information
- **Correct Information**: Facts are accurate, citations are provided when needed
- **Educational Background**: Degrees, certifications, or specialized training
- **Publishing History**: Track record of quality content or recognized publications
- **Credentials**: Professional licenses, accreditations, or formal recognition

### For Mahadaan Website:

- Demonstrate knowledge of yoga philosophy, modern wellness science, and holistic health
- Provide detailed program descriptions with learning outcomes
- Cite traditional texts and modern research where relevant
- Highlight instructor certifications and advanced training
- Show evidence of ongoing professional development

---

## 3. Authoritativeness (A)

Authoritativeness is the **recognition and reputation** of the creator, organization, or website in their field.

### Key Signals:

- **Expert Recognition**: Awards, mentions, or recognition from reputable organizations
- **Backlinks**: Citations from other authoritative sites in the same field
- **Third-Party Reviews**: Positive reviews on trusted platforms (Google Reviews, Trustpilot, etc.)
- **Media Mentions**: Coverage in respected publications or media outlets
- **Professional Endorsements**: Support from other recognized experts
- **Organization Reputation**: The organization's track record and standing in the community
- **About Page**: Clear, detailed information about the organization's mission and authority

### For Mahadaan Website:

- Display third-party endorsements from wellness organizations, yoga associations, or health professionals
- Link to positive reviews and testimonials from reputable sources
- Highlight media features, articles, or mentions in wellness publications
- Show partnerships with established health and wellness organizations
- Create a detailed About section for Antaryog Foundation
- Display awards, certifications, or recognitions received

---

## 4. Trustworthiness (T)

**Trustworthiness is the most important of the four.** It's about confidence that the content is honest, accurate, and free from manipulation.

### Key Signals:

- **Transparency**: Clear ownership, authorship, and disclaimers
- **Secure Website**: HTTPS, no intrusive ads, clean user experience
- **Privacy & Data Security**: Clear privacy policy, data protection practices
- **Fact-Checking**: Accuracy is verifiable; sources are cited
- **Tone & Transparency**: Honest, non-manipulative language; acknowledges limitations
- **User Reviews**: Positive feedback from actual users
- **Contact Information**: Easy way to reach the organization
- **No Misinformation**: Avoids false claims, unsubstantiated health claims, or misleading tactics
- **Ethical Practices**: Honest pricing, clear terms, no deceptive practices

### For Mahadaan Website:

- Display clear contact information and organizational transparency
- Use HTTPS and maintain secure payment processing
- Include comprehensive privacy policy and data protection standards
- Cite sources for health/wellness claims; avoid exaggerated promises
- Display genuine, verified testimonials (ideally from known platforms)
- Be honest about program benefits while managing expectations
- Clearly disclose any affiliations, sponsorships, or partnerships
- Show refund or satisfaction policies transparently
- Regularly update content to reflect current best practices

## Page Quality Ratings Scale

Google's Quality Raters use this scale when evaluating pages:

### Highest

- Exceptional expertise and authority; excellent reputation
- Trustworthy, accurate, and comprehensive content
- Serves user's needs perfectly

### High

- Strong expertise and authority
- Well-researched, accurate information
- Satisfies user intent clearly

### Medium

- Adequate expertise for the topic
- Generally accurate information
- Adequately serves user needs

### Low

- Limited expertise or authority
- May contain inaccurate information
- Partially serves user needs

### Lowest

- Little to no expertise; poor authority
- Untrustworthy or inaccurate
- Does not serve user needs
- May be harmful

---

## Beneficial Purpose Test

Google evaluates whether the page has a **Beneficial Purpose**:

- Does it help users?
- Is it honest and transparent?
- Does it avoid manipulation or deception?
- Is it created with genuine intent to serve the user?

**YMYL content** (finance, health, legal, safety) faces stricter standards.

---

## Needs Met Scale

Raters also evaluate how well a page satisfies user needs:

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Fully Meets** | Fully satisfies the user's search intent |
| **Highly Meets** | Excellent, comprehensive coverage of the topic |
| **Moderately Meets** | Good coverage; most needs are satisfied |
| **Slightly Meets** | Limited coverage; some needs are satisfied |
| **Fails to Meet** | Does not satisfy the user's search intent |

## Best Practices for EEAT Implementation

1. **Invest in Author Credibility**: Highlight team credentials prominently
2. **Build Backlinks**: Collaborate with recognized wellness/yoga organizations
3. **Gather Reviews**: Encourage satisfied students to leave reviews on Google and other platforms
4. **Be Transparent**: Share your story, mission, and values openly
5. **Maintain Accuracy**: Fact-check all claims; cite sources
6. **Secure Your Site**: Use HTTPS, maintain privacy protections
7. **Create Depth**: Publish comprehensive, detailed content on key topics
8. **Stay Current**: Update content regularly to reflect new information
9. **Serve Users First**: Focus on genuine user benefit, not just ranking
10. **Ethical Practices**: Avoid deceptive marketing, false claims, or manipulation
