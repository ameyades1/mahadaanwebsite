01

Introduction

A Divine Act of Service and Blessings

"धर्मस्य मूलं दयाः"

("The root of Dharma is compassion.")

In Sanatan Dharma, Gau Mata is revered not merely as an animal but as the divine mother—a source of abundance, purity, and nourishment. By protecting and serving her, we preserve Dharma, invite divine blessings, and ensure a future of spiritual prosperity. Every great civilisation has been built on the foundations of service to and protection of cows. Now, it is our responsibility to continue this sacred duty.

02

Why Gau Seva?

A Path to Dharma, Prosperity, and Divine Grace

Scriptural Significance of Gau Seva

📖 Atharva Veda (10.10.34) – "The divine cow grants wealth, nourishment, and wisdom to her devotees."
📖 Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (6.4.18) – "One who serves cows with devotion attains all auspiciousness and spiritual progress."
📖 Garud Puran – "Protecting and nurturing cows cleanses past sins and ensures a blissful afterlife."

Why This Mission Needs You

Despite thousands of years of reverence, millions of cows are abandoned, starved, or left untreated. Temples and sacred spaces require sustained support to provide for the cows under their care.

Now is the time to take direct action—to preserve the divine energy of Gau Mata and uphold our sacred duty.
